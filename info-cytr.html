    Testing

    Cost of HIV Testing in India:

    A study conducted in 2005 in Andhra Pradesh showed that the cost of complete VCT services to each HIV-positive and negative person was Rs123.5 (US$2.54) and Rs59.2 (US$1.22), respectively. Quality Assurance and Quality Control are an integral part of HIV testing. NACP III has focused on the assurance of quality in kit evaluation and assessment of HIV testing services through implementation of an External Quality Assessment Scheme (EQAS). In 2000, NACO launched the National External Quality Assessment Scheme (NEQAS) to ensure standard quality of the HIV tests being performed in the program. Read more

    Labs [Laboratory Testing Procedures]

    1. Pre-test Counseling:

      Patients are educated about the testing process, the meaning of potential results, and confidentiality. Consent is obtained.

    2. Sample Collection:

      Blood samples are the most common. Oral fluid or urine samples can also be used.

    3. Processing and Analysis:

      Samples are processed and analyzed in the lab using specific reagents and protocols. Automated systems are often used for high-throughput testing.

    4. Result Interpretation:

      A reactive result in an initial screening test usually requires confirmation with a follow-up test. Western blot or immunofluorescence assay (IFA) might be used for confirmation

    5. Post-test Counseling:

      Patients receive their results. Counseling is provided to discuss the implications of the results, preventive measures, and treatment options if necessary.
